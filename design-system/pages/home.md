# Home Page Override

> Overrides `design-system/MASTER.md` for the Lattice Visual home page.
> Rebuilt to benchmark `https://inmywork.com/` after the previous aesthetic was rejected.

## Page Intent

The homepage is a service conversion page, not a pure portfolio landing page. It must quickly explain the offer, show trust, expose indicative pricing/turnaround and make quote submission feel easy.

## Reference Translation

Use inmywork's home structure:

- Hero with plain offer and immediate lead/order action.
- Early experience/trust section.
- "We design scientific illustrations" explanation.
- Service cards with delivery time and price range.
- Recent journal cover / graphical abstract proof.
- Resource/lead magnet section.
- Testimonials.
- FAQ.
- Final "easy to get started" CTA.

Upgrade the visual execution:

- Cleaner typography and spacing.
- No stock-office hero dominance.
- No plugin-carousel feel.
- No unexplained journal logos.
- More complete artwork display.
- Modern but restrained motion.

## Section Order

1. Hero / quote entry
2. Experience and trust proof
3. We design scientific illustrations
4. Services with price and delivery time
5. Recent work
6. Templates and downloads
7. Resource / free brief template placeholder
8. Testimonials
9. FAQ
10. Final CTA

## Hero

Desktop:

- 12-column layout.
- Left 5 columns: H1, short value statement, mini quote form.
- Right 7 columns: portfolio artwork montage or large image-led panel.
- Header overlays or sits above hero, but must remain readable.

Hero copy direction:

- H1: "Scientific illustrations and academic journal cover designs."
- Subcopy names graphical abstracts, scientific figures, journal covers and animations.
- Mini form fields: name, email, institution/company, service type.
- Primary CTA: `Request a quote`.
- Secondary CTA: `View gallery`.

Mobile:

- H1 and CTA first.
- Mini quote form remains visible but compact.
- Artwork appears immediately after CTA, not buried below several sections.

## Trust Section

Do not use Nature/Cell/ACS/RSC/Wiley logos unless we have real, approved credits and label them clearly.

Allowed proof modules for now:

- "Journal-ready files"
- "Researcher-led briefing"
- "High-resolution delivery"
- "Confidential review workflow"
- "Vector, 300dpi and editable formats"

If client/institution logos are supplied later, label the strip as:

- `Selected client affiliations`
- or `Researchers from these institutions have commissioned similar work`

Never present placeholder logos as real endorsements.

## Services / Pricing

Use service cards similar in function to inmywork:

- Graphical Abstract & Scientific Illustration
- Scientific Journal Cover
- Scientific Animation
- Biology & Medical Illustration
- Paper Figures / Mechanism Diagrams
- Conference Posters / 3D Visualization

Each card must show:

- Delivery time
- Indicative price range
- 1-2 line description
- Get Started CTA

Pricing may be marked `From` or `Indicative` to avoid overpromising.

## Templates / Downloads

Add a homepage section for free and paid scientific templates:

- Position after services or recent work.
- Title: `Templates for faster scientific figures.`
- Show 3-4 featured templates.
- Include both `Free` and `Paid` labels.
- Free cards link directly to downloadable files.
- Paid cards link to `/contact` or `/templates` until payment is implemented.
- CTA: `Browse templates`.

## Visual Rules

- Light theme first.
- Research green primary CTA.
- Soft grey bands can separate major sections.
- Use full-width service bands and grids, not nested cards inside cards.
- Artwork frames use contain by default for figures and graphical abstracts.
- Journal cover images may crop only if the full version appears nearby or on detail page.

## Motion

- Hero fields and artwork can enter with subtle stagger.
- Service cards: border accent + small translate on hover.
- Recent work: image fade/scale no more than 1.02.
- FAQ: 180-240ms height/opacity transition.
- Reduced motion: static.

## Acceptance Criteria

- First viewport communicates service and quote path.
- Pricing/turnaround appears on the homepage before the user reaches the gallery.
- No unexplained journal logo strip.
- At least one strong artwork proof block above or near the first viewport.
- Homepage feels like a scientific illustration service site, not a generic design agency or pure portfolio.
